My Morning Routine:

Organizing My Morning for a More Productive Day

a daily blog by Maria Usina

Today is

My morning routine can make or break the rest of my day, so I’ve developed a little "morning routine checklist" that helps me get organized and be more productive!

I’ve been on a little habits kick lately. I feel like the older I get, the more I’m realizing how much my habits determine how I spend my time and whether or not I achieve my goals. And one of the most important sets of habits that affects how my day plays out is my morning routine.

If I’m a mess in the morning, I feel like I spend the rest of the day trying to catch up. If I am able to be intentional about my mornings, though, the rest of my day tends to go more smoothly as well.

Because of this, I’ve developed a little “morning routine checklist” to help me stay on track. Everyone will have a different morning routine that works for them, their circumstances, and their stage of life, but I thought I’d share what’s working for me these days– I know I’m always interested to know what’s working for other people!

Keep my phone somewhere other than on my nightstand

I realized a while ago that having my phone plugged in right beside the bed on my nightstand was way too big of a temptation for me. I would end up staying up later browsing social media aimlessly, and the first thing I would do in the morning was grab my phone and start browsing aimlessly again… not the best way to start or end my day. To break this habit, I began plugging in my phone somewhere other than by my bed.

Set a positive focus from the start

When my alarm goes off, I quickly put on my workout clothes. Before I actually work out, though, I sit in my cozy chair and start out my day by setting a positive focus. For me this means writing in my journal, and meditating before I do anything else. When I do this first thing, it doesn’t mean that I automatically have a perfect day, but I do feel like I am better equipped to handle whatever may come my way that day.

Get moving

Next I do some sort of short workout using the big, open space in our living room. Right now I am loving World Class on Youtube because it gives me access to a bunch of different workouts right on my big TV. My favorites are EXPRESS L.A.B and Streaching.

Feed my brain

Often if I’m doing a workout that I’m pretty familiar with, I’ll often listen to an audiobook or podcast at the same time. (I love The testing show podcast, and right now I’m listening to 7 Habits of Highly Effective People on Audible). This helps me multi-task a little bit and work out + learn something new at the same time!

Feed my belly

After I’m done working out, I head to the kitchen to make breakfast. I am a creature of habit, so I typically have the same things each day: a glass of celery juice and an avocado toast. Breakfast time also gives me a few minutes to chat with my husband before we head our separate ways for the day.

Attach a new habit to an existing habit that’s already formed

This was a little trick I learned recently that has helped me remember to take my vitamins, of all things! I was always forgetting them, so I needed a way to make sure I took them every single day. Since I was already consistently making myself a cup of tea, I simply put my vitamins in the drawer with my tea collection to automatically remind me to take them while I was making my tea. It has worked like a charm ever since!

Have the tools I need to get ready for the day organized and ready to go

After we finish breakfast, I head upstairs to grab a shower and get ready for the day. This process goes a lot more smoothly if my makeup and hair tools are organized and easy to find.

My little makeup station in my closet is still working really well for me, and I keep my vanity drawers organized so I can find everything I need to get ready.

Now, does this routine happen perfectly every single day? Nope. But if I slack off or get out of the routine, I give myself grace and restart the next day. My morning routine has also changed and evolved over time. It’s a process that I'm always examining and working on getting better at in order to create smooth mornings!

60-minute morning routine template:

  • 7:00am: Wake up, drink a glass of water, and make the bed
  • 7:05am: 15-minute express workout
  • 7:25am: Tea and breakfast, reading today's news
  • 7:35am: Get ready for the day. Shower + makeup
  • 8:00am: Out the door
